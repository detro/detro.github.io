<strong>Today, 10th of September 2008</strong>, the final, complete, clock and anti-clock wise test of the [Large Hadron Collider](http://lhc2008.web.cern.ch/lhc2008) was a major success. The [LHC](http://lhc2008.web.cern.ch/lhc2008/)is working and ready to start to "crash protons"... and probably [<em>blow up your (our all) mind</em>](http://www.detronizator.org/2008/09/06/large-hadron-collider-goes-rap/) ;) .


![LHC Cooldown Status](http://hcc.web.cern.ch/hcc/file/field_lhc.png)

<em>4.5K-1.9K: brrrrrrr</em>


Some interesting links (that are updated on a frequency of 15/30 minutes):

* [LHC Cooldown Status](http://lhc.web.cern.ch/lhc/Cooldown_status.htm) - It shows the temperatures in the different sectors. While they were testing it was on an average of <strong>1.9k!!! O_o</strong>

* [BBC Radio 4 following the event](http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio4/bigbang/updates.shtml#update) on the ground from the Control Room of the LHC
