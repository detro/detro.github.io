In the last period I'm spending some time working with [Google AppEngine](http://appengine.google.com): if you don't know what is it, be ashamed! :P

I would like to highlight a couple of interesting resources that can make the life of who works with this <strong>framework</strong>/<strong>hosting platform</strong>/<strong>cloud computing system</strong> easier:

	* [Google AppEngine Cookbook](http://appengine-cookbook.appspot.com/) with a lot of good recipe (there are few now, written by the Googlers, but I'm sure it's gona blow up soon)

* [The App Gallery](http://appgallery.appspot.com/), with a lot of interesting applications already in production phase

* A set of [Articles](http://code.google.com/appengine/articles/) on "how to do this and that" (sort of little CookBook)

	* The [Django Documentation about Templates (v0.96)](http://www.djangoproject.com/documentation/0.96/templates/): the Template engine used in AppEngine (a superb one, I must say)

* [The official Project Blog](http://googleappengine.blogspot.com/), with news, tips, tricks and other stuff

It was long time I was planning to learn [Python](http://www.python.org/): AppEngine looked like the right way to do so and jump on the [Guido](http://www.python.org/~guido/)'s carriage.

What I'm working on? Nothing special, nothing comples, nothing huge. This time, <strong>Simplicity</strong> is the keyword. ;-)
