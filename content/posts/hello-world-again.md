This is my first post from a whole new backend. Yes, I'm leaving the underperforming and unreliable [Bluehost Hosting](https://www.bluehost.com/) for a scalable, distributed, load-balanced, reliable, powerful platform. I'm going Google. I'm moving to [App Engine](http://code.google.com/appengine/).

To make App Engine serve my purpose, I picked a small, cute, interesting project to do the job: [Bloggart](http://github.com/Arachnid/bloggart), made by brilliant [Arachnid (Nick Johnson)](http://blog.notdot.net/).

The old posts you ask? Yes, I'm working on a strategy for that. Most probably will work a bit on Bloggart to welcome my [Wordpress](http://wordpress.org/)'s data, and use the [App Engine Bulk Loader](http://code.google.com/appengine/docs/python/tools/uploadingdata.html).
Of course, there will be quite few bumps on the way: Bloggart is not Wordpress. I'm sure there will be tons of features that will be left out. But Bloggart and App Engine suite my primary purpose: Speeeeeed. In fact, I recommend you take a good look at [this series of article on Nick's blog](http://blog.notdot.net/2009/10/Writing-a-blog-system-on-App-Engine) where he goes step-by-step through the development of his project: really cool stuff.

And now, back to coding: there is **> 6 years** worth of blogging to import!
