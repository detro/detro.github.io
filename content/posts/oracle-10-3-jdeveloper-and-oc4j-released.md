![OTN Logo](http://www.oracle.com/admin/images/otn/otn_logo_small.gif)
I'm not an [Oracle developer](http://www.oracle.com/technology/index.html). I'm not an Oracle DB Manager. I'm not an Oracle Java Developer.
I'm an Eclipse lover since [Eclipse 3.0M*](http://www.eclipse.org) releases but, because of the power of Oracle, I had ever followed our development in the Java-Development-World.

5 days ago I Oracle sent to me information the release of [JDeveloper 10.3](http://www.oracle.com/technology/products/jdev/collateral/1013newfeatures.htm) and [OC4J 10.3 (Oracle Container for Java - our Application Server)](http://www.oracle.com/technology/tech/java/oc4j/index.html), and today I spent a little amount of time looking at the [new features of JDeveloper](http://www.oracle.com/technology/products/jdev/collateral/1013newfeatures.htm) and looking the interesting [flash-screencast-demos](http://www.oracle.com/technology/products/jdev/viewlets/viewlet.html).

Obviously, these product are available for Linux, Windows, MacOSX and... any Java-Enabled systems.

It seems to be very interesting software: ok ok, it's not  [Open Source](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_source)... but it's [Free (of cost)](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gratis) and it's very powerfull.
I <strong>strongly</strong> suggest to give it a chance!.

And, if you don't know it, Oracle is an [Eclipse Add-In Provider](http://www.eclipse.org/membership/members/add_in_provider.php) and it's not impossible to have a plugin Eclipse produced directly from Oracle.

Update: [Oracle's ADF Faces donation to Apache](http://www.oracle.com/technology/products/jdev/htdocs/faq_adffaces_apache.html?msgid=4589089). Oracle is not completelly indifferent respect the OpenSource. It's not?
