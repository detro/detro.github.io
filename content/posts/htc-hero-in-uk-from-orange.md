I'm with a bit of proudness that I say that in July the [HTC Hero](http://www.htc.com/www/product/hero/overview.html), a new Android-based smart-phone, is going to be launched.

For UK, [Orange will be the operator to sell it](http://newsroom.orange.co.uk/2009/06/24/orange-uk-introduces-first-android-handset-to-its-device-portfolio-with-the-introduction-of-the-htc-hero/). <del datetime="2009-06-24T19:22:15+00:00">As far as I know, details are not being disclosed yet.</del>

HTC has developed this new [UI](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User_interface)/[UX](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User_experience_design) ([User Interface](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User_interface)/[User eXperience](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/User_experience_design)) on the top of [Android](http://www.android.com), called "<strong>HTC Sense</strong>". Some says, on the top of [Android (2.0 codename "Donut")](http://www.google.com/search?client=safari&rls=en-us&q=android+donut&ie=UTF-8&oe=UTF-8). I personally don't know if it's true: if it is soon we will have it in the Android branch.
<em>But something tells me that this is going to be ONLY available on Android HTC phones. It's too much of a "added value" for HTC to give it out to everyone else</em>.

Another most notable thing, this will be the first phone on the market to have full support for [Adobe Flash](http://www.adobe.com/products/flashplayer/) (not just [Flash Lite](http://www.adobe.com/products/flashlite/) ;) ). [Adobe announces it here](http://www.adobe.com/devnet/devices/articles/htchero.html).
