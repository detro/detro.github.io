![EMI logo](http://www.blogsmithmedia.com/www.tuaw.com/media/2007/04/emilogo.png)
Every (almost) web site is say the same thing: today is a big day for the freedom (of the music and the customers). [The Jobs's tought is now reality](http://www.detronizator.org/2007/02/07/drm-and-jobs/).
[en:EMI] (in the person of the CEO, Eric Nicoli) and [en:Steve_Jobs|Jobs], in a live blogging on [Engadget](http://www.engadget.com/2007/04/02/steve-jobs-and-emi-present-an-exciting-new-digital-offering/), announce that, starting on May 2007, <strong>[iTunes](http://www.apple.com/itunes/) (and, probably, any other EMI music reseller) will sell music without any DRM</strong>.

Details can be found on:
- [Engadget](http://www.engadget.com/2007/04/02/steve-jobs-and-emi-present-an-exciting-new-digital-offering/)
- [TUAW](http://www.tuaw.com/2007/04/02/its-official-drm-free-music-from-emi/) ([here about pricing and upgrading of already bought music](http://www.tuaw.com/2007/04/02/emi-drm-free-tracks-on-itunes-pricing-and-upgrading/))
- [Melablog](http://www.melablog.it/post/3496/conferenza-stampa-emi-diretta) (and [here](http://www.melablog.it/post/3497/la-fine-del-drm))

Only other 2 words: 

* the quality is higher (256kb/s) but also the price ($1.29 per song). Instead, the price of the videos will remain the same ($1.99 per video). Not so good for the little wallets, but the first step in the good direction.

* And, what about other majors? Actually, no other majors has joined "<strong>this big step forward</strong>" but, as Jobs has said a lot of time, &laquo;<strong><em>Someone makes history, the others simply follow</em></strong>&raquo;.
