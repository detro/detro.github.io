Those experiments are collected here: [Chrome Experiments](http://www.chromeexperiments.com/) (note the subtitle: "<em>Not your mother's JavaScript</em>" - LOL).

Bear in mind: this is <em>not only</em> because of [Chrome](http://www.google.com/chrome/) itself. This is the power of [HTML5 Canvas element](http://www.whatwg.org/specs/web-apps/current-work/#the-canvas-element), plus, of couse, the very (very) fast [V8 Javascript engine](http://code.google.com/p/v8/)). And indeed, I ran them on my [Safari 4 beta](http://www.apple.com/safari/whats-new.html) without a glitch: the Javascript engine, [SquirrelFish Extreme (SFX)](http://webkit.org/blog/214/introducing-squirrelfish-extreme/) is pretty good too.

I knew [HTML5](http://www.whatwg.org/specs/web-apps/current-work/) is gonna be BIG, but I never thought "that BIG". Not in terms of <em>"it's not doable"</em> but in terms of<em> "who can be that mad to do it!!!"</em> :D
Evidently, there are a lot of "maddos" out there ;)
