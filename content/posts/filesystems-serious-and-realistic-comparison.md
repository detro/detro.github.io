[Nemo translated (in Italian, of course)](http://blog.neminis.org/linux-filesystems-a-confronto.xhtml) an interesting comparison between the most diffused filesystems on Linux (and not only on The Penguin): [Ext2](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ext2)/[3](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ext3), [Reiserfs 3](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reiserfs), [XFS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/XFS), [JFS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JFS).

[The original article](http://www.debian-administration.org/articles/388) was posted by [hansivers](http://www.debian-administration.org/users/hansivers) on the [Debian Administration Website](http://www.debian-administration.org/).

Conclusion? <strong>XFS rulez!!!</strong>
And I'm on the same: I start to use [XFS](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/XFS) average 2 years ago... and some big partitions on my HD still demonstrate optimal performance.
Good Job, [SGI](http://oss.sgi.com/projects/xfs/)!
