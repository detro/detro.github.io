And... seems to be a nice meeting. ;)
After a lot of development, the comunity surrounding the project realeases [last stable version 1.2.3.1](http://www.go-mono.com/archive/1.2.3.1/) of [Mono](http://www.mono-project.com/).

What about [en:Mac_OS_X|Mac OS X]? [Here](http://www.mono-project.com/Mono:OSX) is a [dedicated page on the official wiki of Mono](http://www.mono-project.com/Mono:OSX). And, in particular, I'm happy to highlight a [tutorial/howto about XCode and Mono integration](http://mono-project.com/CSharpPlugin): [CSharpPlugin](http://mono-project.com/CSharpPlugin).

I know that [en:Mono_(software)|Mono] is the open-source implementation of [en:.NET_Framework|.Net] (started by [en:Novell]), and that I hate [en:Microsoft|M$], but... I can't hate a framework. It's like to hate the [en:Plutonium] because we make the [en:Atomic_Bomb] with it! ;)

Besides:
	

* I have had a little experience with Mono (a [en:Gecko_(layout_engine)|Gecko#] and [en:GTK_Sharp|GTK#] based browser)... and it was very good. In particular, I like delegates very much.

	* .Net and [en:C_Sharp|C#] specification are standardized at the [en:Ecma_International|ECMA]: it's one of the few times that M$ does things in the right way.

Source, [TUAW](http://www.tuaw.com/2007/02/20/mono-allows-net-and-vb-apps-on-the-mac/).
